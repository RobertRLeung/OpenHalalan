About the project

Data para sa bayan.

Doing research on the Philippines is hard. Finding the data doesn't have to be. OpenHalalan started as a byproduct of an undergraduate thesis on Philippine political dynasties. It's non-partisan, volunteer-run, and independent — no party, campaign, or government office funds or controls it. One bundled, citable dataset: every winning candidate for seven local & district offices since 2004, plus full candidate-level vote counts since 2016 — winners and losers.

Data quality

Known issues.

  • No vote counts from 2013 back. 2013 has no recoverable municipality-level source at all; 2010 exists only as winners-only PDFs covering 83% of towns.
  • Overseas absentee votes are not in the dataset. Every total is a sum of the localities inside the Philippines, so nationwide races run slightly under COMELEC's official canvass — Duterte's 2016 presidential total comes to 15.85M here against an official 16.60M. Local races are unaffected.
  • 2022's national races are withheld pending a re-scrape. The original 2022 scrape was truncated: it captured 7 of the 10 presidential candidates and none of the party-list race — Leni Robredo, who finished second with roughly 15M votes, is missing from it entirely.

Spot something else? See the audit log or let us know.
