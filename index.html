About the project

Data para sa bayan.

Researching the Philippines is hard. Finding the data doesn't have to be. OpenHalalan started as a byproduct of an undergraduate thesis on Philippine political dynasties. It's non-partisan, volunteer-run, and independent: no party, campaign, or government office funds or controls it. One bundled, citable dataset covering every winning candidate for seven local & district offices since 2004, plus full candidate-level vote counts since 2016, winners and losers alike.

Dynasties

7,000 islands, 7,000 dynasties?

Each jurisdiction is shaded by the share of its winning candidates who belong to a political family: one surname holding more than one seat. Click a province to see its towns, or any place to see the families.

Thin · sunud-sunod

Succession over time. The family holds a seat across different years, e.g. a mayor is succeeded by a sibling, a spouse, or a child.

Fat · sabay-sabay

Concurrent office-holding. The family holds two or more seats in the same place in the same year, e.g. the mayor and vice-mayor are brothers.

Dynastic share

    Hover a place for its dynastic share. Click to see the families.

    Dynastic measures describe surname and middle-name co-occurrence in office. Many Filipino surnames are common (e.g. Cruz). We cannot always separate coincidental surnames from real kinship. The map defaults to surname-only matching, the more conservative reading; the toggle adds middle-name links, which capture marriage and maternal ties. Name matching may be inaccurate. No attribution of wrongdoing is intended. Towns cover 2016 to 2025; provinces and regions, 2004 to 2025. See Data quality.

      Data quality

      Known issues.

      • No vote counts before 2016. 2013 has no recoverable municipality-level source, and 2010 survives only as winners-only PDFs covering 83% of towns.
      • Overseas votes are counted, not mapped. They are included in the nationwide totals but have no locality to place on the map. They exist in the data for 2022 only; the other cycles cover domestic localities, so their nationwide totals run slightly under COMELEC's official canvass. Local races are unaffected.
      • Middle names are incompletely recorded. The middle name is a maiden name, so the marriage and maternal links in the dynasty map are strong where it is present and largely missing where it is not.
      • Four map outlines are slightly off. The eight BARMM Special Geographic Area municipalities are drawn from their 2020 interim boundaries, so four of them are one barangay out. Their vote totals are unaffected.

      Spot something else? See the audit log or let us know.
